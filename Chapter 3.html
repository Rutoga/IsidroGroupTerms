Chapter 3
Networking Media

Physical Connection

Connecting expansion card to a network

Thinnet Cable

A flexible coaxial cable about 0.64 centimeters (0.25 inches) thick

Thicknet Cable

A relatively rigid coaxial cable about 1.27 centimeters (0.5 inches) in diameter.

BNCT Connector

This connector joins the network interface card (NIC) in the computer to the network cable.

BNC Barrel Connector

This connector is used to join two lengths of thinnet cable to make one longer length.

Bandwidth

Refers to the measure of the capacity of a medium to transmit data.

Work Area

Where computer workstations and other user devices are located
